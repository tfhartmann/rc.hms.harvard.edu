Research Computing News

January 23, 2015

  • 3/6/15: We have moved back to Gordon Hall 500!
  • The HMS Data Center Move/Network Upgrade is complete! We started the Core and Campus Network upgrades planned for 2015.
  • We are teaching computing and bioinformatics classes this semester: User Training. Unfortunately, all classes are full. Stay tuned for more classes.
  • Welcome to new Research Computing Consultant Jimi Chu

Our Computers Have Moved!

HMS IT, with help from many people across the Quad, recently completed a major Data Center project. We moved into a new consolidated data center space at 1 Summer Street in Boston with a new and significantly upgraded network. This was the first step in a large infrastructure project to upgrade and improve the HMS Data Center, Data Storage and all parts of the HMS Network. Improvements to the Quad network and network core are now underway. Stay tuned for more information!



New Classes Starting January!

We have started another round of computing and bioinformatics classes this January. We have several new offerings this semester. Classes are listed on our User Training wiki page. The classes and waiting lists were filled quite quickly, so we will repeat them later in the year.



New Research Computing Consultant


Jimi Chu


We are happy to announce that Ruilin (Jimi) Chu has joined the group as a Research Computing Consultant. Jimi has a PhD in Computational Condensed Matter Physics. He brings experience with various programming languages, parallel/cluster computing, and Matlab.



Biogrid Applications



Research Computing is working with SBGrid (www.sbgrid.org) to develop new ways to manage the complex software stack used by many researchers at HMS. A new way to access the applications available on Orchestra called BioGrids is currently in development. An “alpha” version is available for testing on Orchestra. The collection overlaps with software already on Orchestra, but it will be maintained by the BioGrids Initiative and will be rapidly expanding to offer comprehensive support. See the BioGrids on Orchestra wiki for more details, and send feedback or comments to orchestra_apps@biogrids.org. BioGrids is supported by the HMS TnT fund and based upon the SBGrid.org support model.
